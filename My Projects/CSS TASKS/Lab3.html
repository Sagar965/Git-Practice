Floating Image Example

This paragraph wraps around the image that is floated to the left. The float property takes the element out of the normal document flow and places it to the left or right of its container. Other content then flows around it. This is a common technique for text wrapping around images in articles or blog posts. It's important to clear floats when they are no longer needed to prevent layout issues.

Main Content Area

This is the main content area of our two-column layout. The sidebar is positioned absolutely, and the main content has a left margin to make space for it. Absolute positioning takes an element completely out of the document flow, positioning it relative to its closest *positioned* ancestor. If no positioned ancestor exists, it's relative to the initial containing block (usually the `` element).
